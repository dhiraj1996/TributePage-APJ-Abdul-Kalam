Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

He came to be known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.

Here's a timeline of Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam:

  • 1931 - Born as Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam on October 15 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1954 - Graduates in Physics from Saint Joseph’s College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1960 - Earns a degree in Aeronautical Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology.
  • 1969 - Transfers to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) from the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), where he plays a pivotal role in India’s civilian space program and military missile development efforts.
  • 1980 - Leads the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program (IGMDP), resulting in the successful development of Prithvi, Agni, and Trishul missiles.
  • 1981 - Kalam recieves the Padma Bhushan for his contribution to the field of science and technology.
  • 1990 - Kalam is awarded the Padma Vibhushan, India’s second highest civilian honour
  • 1997 - Honoured with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.
  • 1998 - Plays a crucial role in India’s Pokhran-II nuclear tests, demonstrating India’s nuclear capabilities.
  • 1999-2001 - Serves as the 1st Principal Scientific Adviser to the Government of India.
  • 2002 - Elected as the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007.
  • 2003 - Interacts with students across the country, inspiring them with his vision for a developed India through his book “Wings of Fire”..
  • 2005 - Proposes the concept of “Vision 2020”, envisioning India as a developed nation by the year 2020..
  • 2006 - Addresses students at the European Parliament, emphasizing the importance of youth and education.
  • 2007 - Concludes his term as President, leaving a lasting legacy as the “People’s President”.
  • 2011 - Launches the “What Can I Give?” movement, encouraging selfless service and patriotism.
  • 2015 - Passes away on July 27 in Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of scientific excellence and inspirational leadership.
  • Memorial - The Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Memorial is established in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, honoring his contributions.
  • Books - His writings, including “Ignited Minds”, “Indomitable Spirit”, and “Transcendence”, continue to inspire generations.

Dr. Kalam’s unwavering commitment to science, education, and national development remains an enduring inspiration for all. 🚀🇮🇳
